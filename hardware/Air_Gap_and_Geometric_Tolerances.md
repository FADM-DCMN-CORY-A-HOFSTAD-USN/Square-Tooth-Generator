I. The Air Gap Calculation ($L_{AG}$)To achieve the sharp magnetic cut-off required for a pure square wave at high frequencies (400 Hz to 1,000 Hz), we must maximize the Permeance Coefficient while maintaining safe mechanical clearance.Assuming a prototype rotor outer diameter ($D_{Ro}$) of 120 mm utilizing high-grade N52 Neodymium magnets:Optimal Static Air Gap ($L_{AG}$): 0.75 mm (0.029 inches).Why 0.75mm? This is the mathematical "sweet spot" for a 12-pole PMSG of this scale. It is tight enough to ensure the magnetic flux density ($B_{AG}$) transitions almost instantaneously across the $30^\circ$ stator cutouts, triggering the 0xFFF high state in the Hex Chip without a latency-inducing ramp-up period.II. High-Speed Mechanical Allowances (10,000 RPM)At 10,000 RPM, the physical metals will deform. The CAD file must accommodate these dynamic forces so the 0.75 mm gap does not close during operation.Centrifugal Rotor Growth: The centrifugal force will cause the N52 magnets and the inner rotor sleeve to expand outward radially. You must build a 0.08 mm radial expansion tolerance into the rotor retaining ring design.Thermal Expansion: High continuous power generation creates eddy current heating in the stator. Assuming an operational temperature delta of $+60^\circ\text{C}$, the stator ring will expand outward (away from the rotor), partially offsetting rotor growth.Bearing Runout: To maintain this sub-millimeter concentricity at top speed, the main shaft cannot exhibit lateral play. The CAD housings for the main shaft must be dimensioned for press-fit, high-speed ceramic bearings to ensure radial runout remains below 0.02 mm.III. CNC Machining DirectivesWhen exporting the .STEP files for manufacturing, append the following strict directives for the machine shop:Stator Inner Diameter (ID) Tolerance: $+0.02 \text{ mm} / -0.00 \text{ mm}$Rotor Outer Diameter (OD) Tolerance: $+0.00 \text{ mm} / -0.02 \text{ mm}$Surface Finish: The mating surfaces of the stator teeth and the rotor magnet faces must be machined to a surface roughness of Ra 0.8 or better. Any micro-abrasions or tooling marks will cause localized magnetic flux leakage, which the Hex Chip will misread as a voltage drop.Assembly Protocol: Because the N52 magnets exert extreme attractive forces across a 0.75 mm gap, final assembly of the rotor into the stator core must be performed utilizing specialized alignment jigs.
